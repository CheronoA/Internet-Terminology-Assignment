TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol)

TCP/IP is the fundamental communication protocol suite of the Internet. It defines how data is packaged, addressed, transmitted, routed, and received.

Key facts about TCP/IP:

  • TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) breaks data into packets and reassembles them
  • IP (Internet Protocol) handles addressing and routing packets
  • Developed in the 1970s by Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn
  • Forms the basis of all modern Internet communication
